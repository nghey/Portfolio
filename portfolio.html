At home is where the familiy is

If I must describe the word “home” or “homeland” in one word, the I would set it into relation with “at home”.
“At home” is not necessarily a house, that has a roof and four walls around it, which stands at a specific spot, but it is about the people living with you. It is more like a mere coincidence that there where everybody gathers frequently or there where you meet those people, you call family, the most frequent is in a house. That is why I can say, if we go on a journey to an unknown location, I always take a little piece of home with me even though an unfamiliar feeling accompanies me. “Not at home” is part of the term “home”.
To explain in more detail, it means that you can only feel at home when you are not at home or have not been at home. You must be able to return to a home for it to exist. Therefore, meeting up with friends, for example, is an activity that takes place outside of being at home. This shows that there are different groups of people who affect me in different ways.
Activities outside the home differ from pastimes at home in terms of emotions, because being at home gives me mental peace, i.e. home is a place of security and relaxation, where there is no criticism, judgment or condemnation and you are accepted as you are or want to be. Many of my friends have already moved out, and one day I will too.
The concept of home would develop in different directions, as there are different reasons. If the move is due to studies, work, etc., for example, and this remains a limited or temporary period, this is seen as an activity outside the home, i.e. as a time that one must be away from home in order to define home as such, which at this point corresponds to the parental home here in Regensburg. However, as soon as it is time to start a new life with a steady partner, a new family is founded, a new home is created, a new place to feel comfortable. From this it can be concluded that the family is growing and so is the home, as the feelings are probably of equal importance.

A fusion of different values into a unique relationship

However, some characteristics will certainly be different than they are now.
Simply because my parents experienced a different childhood, history and built a life in Germany under certain circumstances, the conditions and requirements for establishing a new home are different. Many compromises were made, some of which were necessary to adapt to the different cultures, as my mother is half Russian and half Kazakh and belongs to the Protestant faith and my father is half Turkish and half Iraqi/Arab and follows the Islam. For example, no pork is eaten in our household, as Islam forbids it, but at the same time Christianity has nothing against abstaining from pork.
I identify as a German, mostly because of the language, the given mentalities and norms, but I also incorporate cultural aspects from Russia, Kazakhstan, Iraq and Turkey into my everyday life. This also includes the food culture, like daily tea and rice are a must.
Our eating habits have gone so far that the smell of rice has become our own odor, according to the observations of my friends. As a result of numerous compromises, we have created a unique relationship with each other that is connected with unique feelings and thus defines home. The answer "at home" to the question "What is home in one word?" may seem simple, but my "at home" is much more.
